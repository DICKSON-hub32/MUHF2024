About

The Machakos University (MksU) Tech and Engineering Community presents the second MksU Hackfest, focused on "Sustainability in Engineering and Technology Solutions." In partnership with the MksU Innovation Club, Microsoft Learn Students Ambassadors, Google Developer Student Club Machakos University, and Techo-Engineering Association, we offer a platform for students from diverse backgrounds to develop sustainable solutions for real-world challenges.

This dynamic event fosters innovation through the convergence of minds and perspectives. Participants will enhance skills via workshops and mentorships centered on sustainable Technological and Engineering practices, bridging academia and industry.

After a successful inaugural edition, join us again to shape a sustainable future. Be part of something greater, driving positive change through your creativity. MksU Hackfest 2.0 calls for innovation, collaboration, and empowerment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is MksU Hackfest 2.0?

MksU Hackfest 2.0 is a collaborative event focused on "Sustainability in Engineering and Technology Solutions," organized by the Machakos University Tech and Engineering Community. It brings together students to develop innovative solutions for real-world sustainability challenges.

Code of conduct

Read our full Code of Conduct here

Who can participate?

The hackfest is open to all university students, regardless of their academic background. We encourage participation from diverse fields to promote cross-disciplinary innovation.

Do I need to be a technical expert to participate?

No! While technical skills are valuable, we welcome participants from all skill levels. Teams benefit from diverse perspectives, including design, business, and environmental expertise.

What is the event format?

The event includes:

  • Hands-on hackathon sessions
  • Technical workshops
  • Mentorship opportunities
  • Industry expert presentations
  • Networking sessions

Will there be mentorship available?

Yes! Industry professionals and academic experts will be available throughout the event to guide teams and provide valuable insights.

What kind of projects can we work on?

Projects should address sustainability challenges through technology and engineering solutions. This could include:

  • Renewable energy applications
  • Waste management solutions
  • Smart resource management
  • Sustainable agriculture tech
  • Green transportation solutions

Can I participate as an individual?

While we encourage team participation, individual registrations are welcome. We'll help match you with other participants to form balanced teams.

What's the maximum team size?

Teams can have up to 4 members. We recommend having members with diverse skill sets.

What should I bring?

  • Your laptop and charger
  • Student ID
  • Any specific software or tools you prefer working with
  • Enthusiasm and creative ideas

What resources will be provided?

  • Development tools and platforms
  • Cloud credits (courtesy of our partners)
  • Technical documentation
  • Mentorship support
  • Refreshments during the event

Is there any pre-event preparation required?

We recommend:

  • Familiarizing yourself with basic sustainability concepts
  • Reviewing provided learning resources (to be shared after registration)
  • Installing common development tools
  • Joining our pre-event orientation session

What are the judging criteria?

Projects will be evaluated based on:

  • Innovation and creativity
  • Sustainability impact
  • Technical implementation
  • Scalability and feasibility
  • Presentation quality

Who are the organizing partners?

The event is organized in collaboration with:

  • Machakos University
  • Microsoft
  • ALX
  • GDSC
